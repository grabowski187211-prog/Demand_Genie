Version 5 Video Narration

Teleprompter script for the current Demand Genie judging demo.

Target duration: approximately 2 minutes 35 seconds.
Bracketed lines are on-screen cues and are not spoken.
[Start on Replenishment.]

Planning evidence is often split across forecasts, inventory, open orders, and spend reports. Demand Genie brings it together so a planner can see what needs attention, understand why, and decide what to do next.

[Show the replenishment summary and select an exception.]

I start with a ranked replenishment queue. Selecting an exception exposes on-hand inventory, eligible open supply, qualified demand, buffer zones, order rules, and the proposed action.

This is decision support, not an automatic release engine. I can release or hold the proposal, add a reason, and record the decision. The planner stays in control.

[Open Forecast and show model evidence.]

The forecast is not a black box. Demand Genie compares seven candidates on the same rolling-origin backtest, including classical models and TiRex2. It shows the selected model, error, bias, interval, and horizon.

A forecast-driven planning change becomes a review proposal. It never silently overwrites qualified demand or approved buffer settings.

[Show decomposition, then Spend.]

Decomposition separates trend, seasonality, and remaining variation while declaring that three annual cycles are evidence, not certainty.

Spend shows reconciled transactions, concentration, and evidence-backed commercial prompts. These are questions to investigate, not invented or booked savings.

[Open Portfolio and highlight the pattern mix.]

Version 5 gives the 80 synthetic items realistic smooth, erratic, intermittent, and lumpy behavior. Kraljic positioning shows business impact and supply risk, while the segmentation queue connects each pattern to a planning response.

[Briefly show Execution, Buffers, and Data.]

Execution highlights open supply needing intervention. Buffers exposes approved settings, ownership, and review dates. Data is the trust layer, checking inputs, spend reconciliation, forecast provenance, and warnings.

If a workbook does not match the analyzed forecast package, the dashboard switches to labeled browser benchmarks. It never pretends that ARIMA, TiRex2, or robust STL ran in the browser.

[Keep Data visible, then briefly show the README or build log.]

I built Demand Genie with GPT-5.6 and Codex. I made the product and supply-chain decisions: the problem, methods, offline delivery model, and automation boundaries.

GPT-5.6 helped reason across forecasting, DDMRP, procurement, and design. Codex generated data, built five versions, ran the forecast pipelines, tested the browser, and audited the calculations.

That review loop changed the product. It caught provenance and missing-input risks, then traced unrealistic segmentation to the sample data and rebuilt it for Version 5.

Demand Genie turns fragmented planning evidence into clearer, faster, and more accountable human decisions.

Recording note: this script describes the packaged synthetic V5 workbook. Do not claim live ERP integration, automatic purchasing, booked savings, or browser-based TiRex2, ARIMA, or robust STL execution.
